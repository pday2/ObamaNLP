The Intel Corporation announced plans on Friday to build a $5 billion microprocessor plant in Arizona and hire 4,000 employees in the United States this year. The workers will focus on product development, research and design, the chief executive, Paul S. Otellini, said. Mr. Otellini disclosed the plans for the plant during a visit by President Obama to an Intel plant near Portland, Ore. The new plant will be located at the company's existing site in Chandler, Ariz.
